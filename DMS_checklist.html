NIH Data Management and Sharing Plan Checklist

A Data Management and Sharing (DMS) Plan must contain the six elements below. A blank DMS format template can be downloaded here. For example DMS plans you can use the DMS Example Plan Finder tool. Please note that sample DMS Plans may include expectations beyond the general Data Management and Sharing Plan Policy. For common inquiries about DMS plans, see our FAQ.

The checklist below includes general requirements identified on the NIH Data Management & Sharing Plan website. Prior to submission, review the NIH instructions in full for additional elements that may be required for your specific institute or research topic. If directions are unclear on the NIH website, please contact your relevant program officer.

Element 1: Data Type

Summarize the types and amount of scientific data your study will generate or use, including modality, aggregation, and processing level. Identify which data will be preserved and shared, justify your decisions, and include supporting metadata and documentation.

Element 2: Related Tools, Software, and/or Code

Identify tools, software, and code needed to access, manipulate, or interpret the data. Include versions, availability, and access methods.

Element 3: Standards

Describe standards applied to data and metadata, including formats, dictionaries, and other documentation. Use established community standards when possible; if none exist, describe your custom approach.

Element 4: Data Preservation, Access, and Timelines

Outline plans for data preservation, sharing, and access. Specify repositories, access methods, sharing timelines, and long-term stewardship.
